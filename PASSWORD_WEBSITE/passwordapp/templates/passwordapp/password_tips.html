10 Tips to Make and Keep Your Password Safe

Follow these tips to create strong and unique passwords that will keep your online accounts secure.

  1. 1. Use a passphrase instead of a password

    A passphrase is a sequence of words that is easy for you to remember, but hard for others to guess.

  2. 2. Make it long and complex

    Aim for a minimum of 12 characters, including uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.

  3. 3. Avoid common patterns and sequences

    Stay away from common patterns like "qwerty" or "123456", and avoid using the same password for multiple accounts.

  4. 4. Don't use personal information

    Avoid using your name, birthdate, or common words that can be easily guessed by others.

  5. 5. Use a password manager

    Consider using a password manager like LastPass or 1Password to generate and store unique, complex passwords for each account.

  6. 6. Avoid using the same password for multiple accounts

    If a hacker gets access to one of your accounts, they'll have access to all of them if you're using the same password.

  7. 7. Don't share your passwords with anyone

    Keep your passwords confidential and don't share them with friends, family, or coworkers.

  8. 8. Change your passwords regularly

    Consider changing your passwords every 60-90 days to keep your accounts secure.

  9. 9. Use two-factor authentication

    Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) whenever possible to add an extra layer of security to your accounts.

  10. 10. Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity

    Regularly check your account activity and report any suspicious behavior to the relevant authorities.
